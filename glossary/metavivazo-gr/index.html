Metavivazo (Transfer of Ownership)

Metavivazo is the Greek term for the formal transfer of property ownership. It is executed before a notary and must be registered in the Ktimatologio (land registry) to give the buyer full legal title against third parties.

In Greece, property ownership is transferred by a notarial deed (symvolaio metavivasis) — the metavivazo — signed before a licensed notary (symvolaiogrFos). The notary verifies the seller's title, checks for encumbrances, collects the transfer tax (FMA), and certifies the deed. Without a notarial deed, no property transfer is legally valid in Greece.

Before the metavivazo can proceed, the seller must provide a certificate of no outstanding ENFIA (property tax) from the tax authority (AADE), a certificate confirming no outstanding debts to the municipality, and a confirmation from the technical chamber (TEE) that the property has no illegal constructions — or that any such constructions have been regularised. Missing any of these certificates causes the transaction to stall.

The buyer must obtain a Greek tax identification number (AFM — Arithmos Forologikou Mitroou) before the metavivazo can be signed. For foreign buyers this requires a visit to a Greek tax office (DOY) with a passport and proof of address, or it can be done via a lawyer with power of attorney.

Following signing, the notary submits the deed to the Ktimatologio (Land Registry) for registration. The buyer's ownership is only fully enforceable against third parties once the registration is complete — though they are bound by the deed from the date of signing.

Transfer tax (FMA — Foros Metavivasis Akiniton) is levied at 3.09% of the higher of the declared purchase price or the tax-assessed 'objective value' (antikeimeniki axia) of the property. It is collected by the notary on the day of signing.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming a low declared price (to reduce transfer tax) is safe in Greece is wrong. The tax authority uses the 'antikeimeniki axia' (objective value) as a floor — if your agreed price is below it, tax is calculated on the objective value regardless.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Obtain a Greek AFM number well before the target signing date. In some areas the tax office appointment wait is several weeks. Delays in getting the AFM are one of the most common causes of postponed completions for foreign buyers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the antikeimeniki axia?

The government's own 'objective value' for tax purposes — a zone-based formula that sets a floor on transfer tax calculations. If the declared sale price is below this value, transfer tax is calculated on the objective value.

Can I complete a Greek property purchase remotely?

Yes — via a notarial power of attorney (plirexousio) granted to a Greek lawyer. The lawyer then attends the metavivazo on your behalf. The POA itself must be notarised and apostilled in your country.

What is the Ktimatologio?

Greece's national land registry, replacing the older local mortgage registries (Ypothikofilakia) which are still used in areas not yet covered by the Ktimatologio. The Ktimatologio records digitally and its entries prevail in case of conflict.

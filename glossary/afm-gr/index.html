AFM (Greek Tax Identification Number)

The AFM (Arithmos Forologikou Mitroou) is the Greek tax identification number that every property buyer — including foreigners — must obtain before signing any property deed. It is also required to open a Greek bank account.

The AFM (Arithmos Forologikou Mitroou — Tax Registration Number) is Greece's equivalent of a national tax number. Every individual or company that engages in any financial transaction in Greece — including purchasing property — must have one. For foreign buyers, obtaining an AFM is one of the first procedural steps in any Greek property transaction.

Greek residents obtain an AFM automatically through their registration with a local tax office (DOY — Dimosias Oikonomikis Ypiresias). Non-resident foreigners must visit a DOY in person (or instruct a lawyer with power of attorney to do so) and present a valid passport plus a completed M1 form. The AFM is typically issued on the same day.

Without an AFM, a foreigner cannot: sign a Greek property deed, open a Greek bank account, register with the Greek tax authority, or pay property tax (ENFIA). It is the gateway to all formal property and financial activity in Greece.

The AFM is also required for filing annual Greek tax returns, which may be necessary if the buyer earns rental income from their Greek property. Even if no income is earned, buyers who are registered in the Greek tax system may be required to file a nil return annually — confirm with a Greek tax adviser.

Power of attorney to a Greek lawyer can cover the AFM application and all subsequent steps — including attending the metavivazo, registering with the Ktimatologio, paying transfer tax, and opening a bank account. This remote process is well-established and routinely used by international buyers.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Leaving the AFM application until shortly before the signing date is a common mistake. DOY offices in tourist areas can have long queues in summer. Apply at least two weeks before the target completion date.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Grant power of attorney to your Greek lawyer to obtain the AFM on your behalf — this is the most efficient route for foreign buyers and avoids a special trip to Greece just for the tax registration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have more than one AFM?

No. Each individual has exactly one AFM for life. If you already have a Greek AFM from a previous transaction, use the same number — do not attempt to obtain a second one.

Do I need a Greek bank account to buy property?

Not strictly for the purchase itself, but you will need one to pay ENFIA and other ongoing taxes, to receive rental income efficiently, and to handle any future sale proceeds. Open the account early in the process.

Is the AFM the same as a Greek social security number (AMKA)?

No. AFM is the tax number; AMKA (Arithmos Mitroou Koinonikis Asfalistis) is the social security number used for healthcare and employment. Property buyers need the AFM; the AMKA is only required if you plan to reside and work in Greece.

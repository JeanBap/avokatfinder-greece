Ktimatologio (Land Registry)

The Ktimatologio is Greece's national digital land registry that records legal ownership and encumbrances on all properties. Replacing the older Ypothikofilakia system, it provides the authoritative record of who owns what in Greece.

Greece's Ktimatologio (national land registry) is the definitive public register of property rights. Established progressively from 1995, it has replaced the older mortgage registry offices (Ypothikofilakia) in areas where the cadaster has been completed. In areas not yet converted, the Ypothikofilakia remains the active register.

The Ktimatologio records each property with a unique KAEK (Ktimatologikos Arithmos Estias Kai Ktirimatos — Cadastral Number), which identifies the plot and any building on it. Due diligence requires obtaining the Ktimatologio extract (apospasma ktimatologiou diagrAmma) showing boundaries and a certificate of registered rights (pistopoiitiko ktimatologiou) confirming ownership and encumbrances.

One critical Greek-specific risk: properties in areas covered by the Ypothikofilakia (not yet Ktimatologio) may have titles based on notarial deeds but without comprehensive boundary records. This creates a higher risk of boundary disputes, overlapping claims, and forest-encroachment issues, particularly for rural and island properties.

Forest registry (Dasiko Ktimatologio) is a separate and important check in Greece. Properties designated as forest land (dasikes ektaseis) cannot be developed and their development constitutes a criminal offence. Forest map designations are not always obvious from the Ktimatologio alone — a separate check with the local forest service is essential for rural purchases.

Registration of the metavivazo (transfer deed) with the Ktimatologio costs a registration fee of approximately 0.475% of the transaction value, paid by the buyer.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a Greek island or rural property without checking the Dasiko Ktimatologio (forest registry) is a serious error. Forest-designated land cannot be developed under Greek law, and some buyers have discovered their purchased 'plot' is legally forest after completion.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For properties in Ypothikofilakia areas, instruct your lawyer to conduct a 20-year title search going back through successive notarial deeds, not just check the current registration. Gaps in the chain are a real risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the KAEK?

The Ktimatologikos Arithmos Estias Kai Ktirimatos — the unique national cadastral identifier for every property in the Ktimatologio. All official extracts and certificates reference this number.

How do I know if my property is in the Ktimatologio or Ypothikofilakia?

Search at ktimatologio.gr using the address. If the property appears with a KAEK, it is registered in the Ktimatologio. If not, check with the local Ypothikofilakia office.

What is a dasiki ektasi?

Forest land (dasiki ektasi) is land designated as forest by the state, regardless of current tree cover. Greek law prohibits any construction on such land. Properties bordering or partially overlapping forest zones face severe restrictions.

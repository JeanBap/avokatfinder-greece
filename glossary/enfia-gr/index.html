ENFIA (Unified Property Tax)

ENFIA (Eniaios Foros Idioktisias Akiniton) is Greece's annual unified property tax levied on all immovable property owners. It is assessed by the tax authority based on the objective value of each property.

ENFIA was introduced in 2014 as Greece's unified annual property tax, replacing several previously fragmented real estate taxes. Every person or legal entity owning Greek real property on 1 January of the tax year is liable. The tax is assessed automatically by the AADE (Greek tax authority) based on property data and sent as an annual assessment (ekkatharistiko) in late spring.

ENFIA has two components: the principal tax (kyrios foros) calculated on each individual property based on its surface area, location zone, floor, age, and objective value; and the supplementary tax (sympliromatikos foros) levied on individuals whose total Greek property portfolio exceeds EUR 300,000 in objective value, at a progressive rate up to 1.15%.

Payment can be made in up to ten monthly instalments. Non-payment of ENFIA creates a lien on the property and blocks any future transfer — the seller must provide a certificate of zero outstanding ENFIA before any metavivazo (transfer) can be signed by the notary.

ENFIA rates have been adjusted several times since 2014. As of 2023–2024, the government has been gradually reducing rates to stimulate investment, though the supplementary tax on larger portfolios remains significant. Non-residents owning Greek property are subject to the same ENFIA obligations as residents.

Foreign investors using the Golden Visa programme are commonly surprised by their ENFIA bill on a EUR 250,000–800,000 property. On a central Athens apartment near the EUR 800,000 threshold, ENFIA can exceed EUR 5,000 per year depending on the property's specific zone value.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Failing to file a Greek tax return (even a simple one) can result in ENFIA accruing without your knowledge. Foreign buyers must register with a local DOY (tax office) and file annually, even if income from Greece is nil.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Use the AADE portal (aade.gr) to check ENFIA assessments online using your AFM. Review the assessment each year — objective value changes and building attribute errors can cause unexpected increases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I dispute an ENFIA assessment?

Yes. If the property attributes used in the calculation are incorrect (wrong floor, wrong area), you can file an objection with AADE. Supporting documentation from the notarial deed and title certificate is required.

Does ENFIA apply to undeclared properties?

Yes — if AADE identifies undeclared property through the Ktimatologio data, they retroactively assess ENFIA with penalties and interest. Regularising undeclared or inherited property should be done promptly.

Is ENFIA deductible from rental income?

Yes. ENFIA paid on a rental property is deductible from gross rental income for Greek income tax purposes.
